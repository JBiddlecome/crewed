Section 3 — Worksheet A

Regular Withholding Allowances

Each allowance reduces the amount of California income tax withheld. Answer the questions below and we'll calculate your total. If you want maximum withholding, enter 0 for all fields.
Worksheet A — Your Allowances
(a) Personal exemption Enter 1 for yourself. Enter 0 if another person can claim you as a dependent on their taxes.
(b) Blind or over 65 Enter 1 if you are blind. Enter 1 if you are 65 or older. Enter 2 if both apply to you.
(c) Spouse Married (one income) filers only: enter 1 if your spouse has no income from wages or you are the sole earner.
(d) Dependents under 19 or full-time students under 24 Enter the number of qualifying children you will claim.
(e) Other dependents Enter the number of other qualifying dependents (e.g., elderly parents).
(f) Estimated deductions (Worksheet B) Enter allowances from Worksheet B if your expected itemized deductions or credits exceed the CA standard deduction. Leave 0 if unsure — you can always update this form later.
